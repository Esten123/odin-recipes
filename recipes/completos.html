Completos

Description

A Chilean completo is a type of hot dog that is popular in Chile. It's known for being a fully loaded and elaborate version of a hot dog, often considered a Chilean street food delicacy. The completo typically consists of a sausage (similar to a frankfurter or hot dog) served in a soft bread roll called a "marraqueta." What sets the completo apart is its wide array of toppings. A traditional completo is generously topped with ingredients such as diced tomatoes, mashed avocado, sauerkraut, mayonnaise, and sometimes mustard. Some variations may also include chopped onions, ketchup, and other condiments.

Ingredients

  • 1 Chilean hot dog bun (Marraqueta)
  • 1 Beef hot dog
  • 1/4 cup sauerkraut
  • 1/4 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup diced onions
  • 1 tablespoon mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon yellow mustard
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • Hot sauce (optional, to taste)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

Instructions

  1. Slice the Chilean hot dog bun (Marraqueta) horizontally, creating a pocket for the hot dog.
  2. Grill or cook the beef hot dog until it's fully cooked and has a nice char.
  3. Place the cooked hot dog inside the sliced bun.
  4. Layer the sauerkraut, diced tomatoes, and diced onions on top of the hot dog.
  5. In a small bowl, mix together mayonnaise, ketchup, and yellow mustard to create the sauce.
  6. Drizzle the sauce over the toppings on the hot dog.
  7. Add sliced avocado on top of the sauce.
  8. Optional: Add hot sauce for extra spice, and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Close the bun, securing the delicious toppings inside.
  10. Serve and enjoy your authentic Chilean Completo!

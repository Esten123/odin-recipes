Berliner

Description

A Chilean Berliner pastry, known as "Berlines" in Chile, is a delightful and popular treat in Chilean cuisine. It is a type of filled doughnut that is typically round and fried to a golden brown perfection. The outer layer is soft and slightly crispy, creating a delicious contrast with the sweet filling inside.

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • Vegetable oil
  • Filling of your choice: Dulce de leche, fruit jam, chocolate, or pastry cream.

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine warm milk, sugar, and active dry yeast. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until the yeast is activated and the mixture becomes frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture, eggs, and softened butter.
  3. Mix the ingredients together until a dough forms. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes or until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-2 hours or until it doubles in size.
  5. Punch down the risen dough and roll it out on a floured surface to about 1/2-inch thickness. Use a round cutter to cut out doughnut shapes.
  6. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or a large, deep pan to 350°F (175°C).
  7. Fry the doughnuts for 2-3 minutes per side or until they are golden brown. Ensure they are cooked through.
  8. Remove the fried doughnuts and let them cool on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  9. Once the doughnuts are cool, fill them with your preferred filling using a piping bag or another suitable method.
  10. Dust the filled Berliners with powdered sugar for a finishing touch.
  11. Serve and enjoy your homemade Chilean Berliner pastries!
